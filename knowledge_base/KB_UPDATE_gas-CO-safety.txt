## EMERGENCY CALLS

### LIFE-SAFETY HAZARDS — handle BEFORE anything else
If the caller reports a GAS SMELL, a CARBON MONOXIDE alarm, or smoke / a
burning smell, this is NOT a normal service call. Do NOT quote a fee or
offer to dispatch a tech. Tell them: "Please leave the building now and call
911 or your gas company right away." Only after they confirm they are safe
may you offer to schedule a follow-up visit.

### What Counts as an (After-Hours) Emergency
Any of the following qualify for the $120 emergency response:
- Call placed AFTER 5:00 PM Monday through Friday
- Call placed AFTER 2:00 PM on Saturday
- Call placed ANY TIME on Sunday
- During regular hours: no AC at all, no heat at all, water leak from the
  system, flooding, or a burst pipe

### Emergency Protocol (after-hours / no-cooling / no-heat / water)
1. Acknowledge the urgency: "It sounds like this could be an emergency."
2. Inform: "There is a $120 emergency service fee to send a technician out right now."
3. If the customer AGREES to the $120 fee: collect their name, the issue,
   address, phone number, and email. Then transfer the call to the on-call technician.
4. If the customer DECLINES the $120 fee: offer to schedule the next available
   regular appointment instead.
